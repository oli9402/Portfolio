Of course the magic happens in ggplot2.

Likert scales are a very typical way of gathering the opinion of a
sample of customers or population. One way of visualizing the results of
such survey in a fast way are: \textbf{diverging bar plots}

\subsection{When to use?}\label{when-to-use}

This kind of plot is useful if the main interest is the difference in
size between positive and negative response. But if the main interest is
to compare all opinions with each other then diverging bar plots may be
the wrong choice since stacked bars with different starting points are
difficult to compare visually.
